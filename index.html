Welcome!

I am a Visiting Research Assistant Professor in the Department of Mathematics at the University of South Carolina. I recently completed my Ph.D. in the Department of Mathematics at the University of Georgia under the supervision of Danny Krashen.

My research interests include algebra and algebraic geometry. In particular, I am interested in algebraic K-theory, cycle modules and K-cohomology, algebraic cycles, motives and motivic cohomology, derived methods, patching over fields, and homogenous varieties.

This semester (Spring 2017), I am teaching algebraic geometry, focusing on the language and geometry of Grothendieck's theory of schemes.
